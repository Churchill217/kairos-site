When the AI picks your headless CMS, does it name you?

I asked Gemini and Perplexity which headless CMS an indie founder should build on — 24 times, four different ways — and counted who actually got named. The open-source option won the table. But the sites the engines read most were not the products they recommended most, and that gap is the whole story.

What this is

Fewer builders open ten tabs to compare tools now. A lot of them just ask an AI — "what's the best headless CMS for my app?" — read the two or three names it hands back, and shortlist from those. So the real question for anyone selling a CMS isn't "where do I rank on Google." It's when the engine answers, does it ever say my name?

The answers wobble run to run, so one screenshot proves nothing. I asked the same buyer questions 24 times across two engines (Gemini and Perplexity, 2026-07-12) and measured how often each product got named. That frequency is a number almost no founder in this space has ever seen about themselves.

The headline

Strapi — the open-source option — was named in every single answer (100%), ahead of the two big hosted incumbents, Sanity (96%) and Contentful (88%). That makes headless CMS the fourth category I've torn down where the open-source layer out-names the paid leader. It's becoming a pattern: when a developer asks an AI what to build on, the self-hostable name keeps coming out on top.

A credible, tight top three — then Storyblok and Hygraph tie at 75%, Prismic and Payload at 71%, Directus at 62%. Hold onto Hygraph and Prismic; they come back in a minute.

"Visible in AI search" is not one number. It's one number per engine, and the engines disagree hard.

The part founders miss — the pages it reads aren't the products it names

I also logged which web pages the engines actually opened to write these answers. Here's the twist that should stop you. The two most-cited sources of all were hygraph.com (24 times) and prismic.io (23 times) — more than anyone else's. Strapi's own site was cited only 12 times, half as often.

And yet Strapi was named first in every answer, while Hygraph and Prismic sat mid-table at 75% and 71%. Sit with that. The two vendors whose pages the AI read the most were not the two it recommended the most. Being cited — having your comparison posts and docs quoted as a source — is not the same as being recommended. You can win the citation fight (great content, lots of "best CMS" posts) and still lose the naming fight, because the name the model reaches for is the one with the most mindshare across the whole open web, not the one with the best landing page. Getting the AI to read you and getting it to name you are two different games.
