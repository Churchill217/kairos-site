AI-visibility teardown · 2026-07-06

Everyone "knows" LaunchDarkly is the feature-flag default. The AI didn't get the memo.

I asked Gemini and Perplexity which feature-flag tool an indie founder should reach for — 24 times, four different ways — and counted who actually got named. The paid heavyweight everyone assumes is #1 came fourth. Two open-source tools were named in every single answer.

What this is

Ask any engineer what feature-flag service to use and a lot of them will say "LaunchDarkly" on reflex — it's the paid category heavyweight, the safe default. But fewer builders open ten tabs to compare tools now. A lot of them just ask an AI — "what's the best feature-flag tool for my SaaS?" — read the two or three names it hands back, and shortlist from those. So the real question for anyone selling in this space isn't "where do I rank on Google." It's when the engine answers, does it ever say my name?

The answers wobble run to run, so one screenshot proves nothing. I asked the same buyer questions 24 times across two engines (Gemini and Perplexity, 2026-07-06) and measured how often each product got named. That frequency is a number almost no founder in this space has ever seen about themselves.

The headline

Flagsmith and Unleash — both open source — were named in every single answer (100%), ahead of LaunchDarkly (92%), the paid heavyweight everyone assumes is the default. A third open-source tool, GrowthBook, came in at 96%. So the top three names the AI reaches for are all self-hostable, and the tool with the biggest sales team sits fourth.

This was the first category I tore down where open source out-named the paid incumbent — it's since repeated in three more (backend-as-a-service, vector databases, headless CMS). When a developer asks an AI what to build on, the self-hostable name keeps coming out on top. That's not an accident; it's what the open web is written about.

The part founders miss — brand mindshare beats a good docs site

I also logged which web pages the engines actually opened to write these answers. Flagsmith's own site was cited the most (27 times), which lines up — it was also named the most. But look at LaunchDarkly: its site was cited only 13 times, middle of the pack, barely ahead of a random Reddit thread — and it still got named in 92% of answers.

That's the lesson hiding in the data. LaunchDarkly isn't winning the naming fight because the AI reads its docs the most; it's winning on sheer mindshare — years of being "the feature-flag company" baked into everything ever written about the category. Meanwhile the answer itself was assembled from places you don't own: a Reddit thread (cited 15 times), third-party "best feature-flag tools" roundups on octopus.com, cpoclub.com and amoeboids.com — cited as heavily as most vendors' own sites. You can write the best landing page in the world; the engine is quoting the internet's opinion of you, not your marketing copy.
